Work

Circus Circus Reno and The Row, Reno


Midway Games Operator

My first job was at the Circus Circus Midway as a Games Operator. I spent a little more than a year there working in the booth games then moving up into the ticket redemption booth. As a Games Operator, I learned the basics of customer service and what it meant to give someone an experience to remember rather than just doing my job. I invested passion into my work and brought a smile to work every day, some people ended up calling me Smiley. Once being moved into the Redemption Booth, I became one of the two people that kept the games filled with tickets and in working order. Management liked me there since I learned fast and moved fast. I handled large amount of service calls and created a plan with my partner to keep the games going in an efficient way. Building a relationship with management and other departments helped me expanded and offered me an opportunity to transfer in which I spent about six months in the Cage.


Cage Cashier

To expand myself and learn more about the company I worked for, I transferred from the Midway to the Cage. Here, as a cashier, I learned to be much better with money handling and proper procedures for many situations such as a robbery, power outages, and system failures. My time was short as a cashier, but the time spent here was invaluable in my future positions as a Supervisor and Main Banker. I used much of my money handling and customer service skills to help improve my employee's performance by teaching them the proper skills.


Midway Supervisor

After the six months in the Cage, I was offered the opportunity to go back to the Midway as a Supervisor. This was the position I grew to love, I was able to implement change with management and help improve the quality of service we offered on the Midway to give the best experience possible. The hardest part from transitioning from staff to management was overseeing people who were my peers, some being much older than me. I was the young unexperienced supervisor who didn’t know what they were doing but after a while I grew into my role and flourished. Many people started calling me their favorite supervisor, there were three off us, me being the head supervisor of Swing Shift. Although I was appreciated as a supervisor and I loved the position, I ended up leaving in order to have a more flexible schedule and the ability to go part-time while I was in school.


Cage Main Bank Cashier

My most recent position was as a Main Bank Cashier at The Row. Circus Circus Reno was bought out by Eldorado Resorts and then merged the three properties, Eldorado, Silver Legacy, and Circus Circus, into what they now call The Row. From here, I bounced around from each of the three casinos working in the Cage. The Main Bank Cashier is the second in command right under the Supervisor on duty. I would take charge when needed to cover the Supervisor’s break or if the Supervisor was busy. They valued my experience as a Supervisor on the Midway and my experience in the Cage, so I was able to keep the Cage running. Cage management offered me many opportunities to advance in the Cage as a supervisor or transfer to another department more focused into my field, but I declined both offers due to lack scheduling flexibility, as a Supervisor, or scheduling conflicts with my classes, IT department.
